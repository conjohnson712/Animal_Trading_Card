Emperor Penguin

The tuxedo appearance of an Emperor Penguin serves as camoflague while swimming. The white segment camoflagues them from the light above, while the black segment camoflagues them from the darkness below.

  • Scientific Name: Aptenodytes forsteri
  • Average Length: 3.8 Feet (1.1 m)
  • Average Lifespan: 15 - 20 Years
  • Habitat: Antarctica

Emperor penguins are considered the largest penguins in the world, and also hold claim for the deepest dives among penguins. Unlike other species of penguin, Emperors do not build nests; instead, they combine their feet and brood patches to supply warmth to their eggs. Emperors, like many other penguins, will leap out of the water. This action coats them in small air bubbles, allowing for faster aquatic movement upon landing.
